About me

Hello everyone,

My name is Amal Deis, and I am running to be your next DGSS President. As the MBA1 Class Representative, I made a clear promise to listen, to act, and to advocate for my cohort. I delivered on that promise. I strengthened inter-program connection, raised concerns about the barriers non-Francophone students face in recruiting, and worked to ensure that every MBA student felt supported, heard, and represented.

I am now ready to bring that same determination, focus, and follow-through to all Desautels Masters programs.

Our community deserves leadership that is present, informed, and accountable. Leadership that communicates with transparency, creates meaningful collaboration across programs, and transforms student feedback into real solutions. These are the principles that guide my platform: strengthening our community, championing student voices, and boosting student opportunities for everyone at Desautels.

If elected, I will lead with intention, integrity, and action. I will be a President who responds to your needs, represents your voice, and delivers results you can see and feel across Desautels.

Your Voice, My Action.

Thank you.

Why vote for Amal?

  • Proven advocate: Already delivered results as MBA1 Class Rep
  • Community-focused: Initiated inter-program events and collaboration
  • Ready to lead: Strong relationships across programs and faculty

What will you improve if elected?

I will strengthen our community through more inter-program collaboration, amplify student voices by reaching out for feedback, and expand opportunities through career workshops, French-language support, and stronger alumni and industry ties.

  • MBA: Strengthen academic-career balance with focused groups and professional workshops
  • MMA: Friday offs so you don't have to choose between a class or an event
  • MMR: Increased access to alumni mentorship and retail/finance career events

What makes you the best candidate for President?

As MBA1 Representative, I have shown that I can listen, advocate, and take action. I have led initiatives that built stronger connections across programs and ensured that students' voices were heard and acted upon.

As a Montreal native, Graduate McGill alum, and member of the McGill Dobson Network, I bring a deep understanding of our university and a strong professional network that I can leverage to create more opportunities for students.

I am not running on potential; I am running on proven leadership. My record shows that when students raise concerns, I respond and deliver results. That is the kind of visible and action-driven leadership I will bring to strengthen our community, champion student voices, and boost student opportunities.

How will you ensure students are heard?

I believe in leadership that meets students where they are. I will not wait for students to come to me during office hours; I will go to them. My approach is to reach out directly, listen actively, and create open spaces where students feel comfortable sharing their experiences.

For those who prefer privacy, I will establish simple and confidential feedback channels so that everyone can express their concerns safely. Listening means nothing without action, and action is at the core of my leadership. Every voice will be heard, valued, and reflected in DGSS decisions.
